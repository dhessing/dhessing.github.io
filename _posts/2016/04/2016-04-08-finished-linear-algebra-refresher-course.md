I finished my second course on Udacity yesterday, the 
[Linear Algebra Refresher Course](https://www.udacity.com/course/linear-algebra-refresher-course--ud953).
I took the class because it was recommended by Udacity to do before starting the Machine Learning Nanodegree,
but I was a bit disappointed with the content.

The page with the requirements for the Nanodegree mentions the following:

> Intermediate calculus and linear algebra mastery, addressed in the 
> [Linear Algebra Refresher Course](https://www.udacity.com/course/linear-algebra-refresher-course--ud953), including:
>
> - Derivatives
> - Integrals
> - Series expansions
> - Matrix operations through eigenvectors and eigenvalues

But eigenvectors and eigenvalues were not even mentioned.
There was not much theory at all actually.
It was basically a course on programming Gaussian Elimination in python for yourself.
That's OK I guess, but not what I expected.

I later found out that there is a link at the bottom of the requirements section that points to additional *external* sources
on where to learn/refresh all the prerequisites.
So now I guess I'm going to follow some courses on Khan Academy to refresh my skills. 

### Course Review 

The course itself was good and the instructions were clear.
Some of the problems were a bit finicky because they involved manually copying the coordinates over from the video, which was prone to typos.
Frustratingly, there was also a typo in one of the problem sets, which made it impossible to send in a correct solution for the autograder.
Also, there was one file missing from the download section but this one was easily found using a search engine. 

I had some contact with Udacity about fixing the errors in the previous course: Intro to Statistics 
and they said they will try to fix it but that the older courses were not high priority,
so I'm not getting my hopes up for this one.
Also, the forums are down for this course.
