Before starting Machine Learning on Udacity I wanted to do two refresher courses:
[Intro to Statistics](https://www.udacity.com/course/intro-to-statistics--st101) and the
[Linear Algebra Refresher Course](https://www.udacity.com/course/linear-algebra-refresher-course--ud953).
Today I finished the first one. Below are my impressions of the course.

I liked the lectures a lot and I just found out they were given by one of the founders (Thrun) himself.
The lessons have a clear goal of teaching you intuition over math.
I think that's great because all the calculations are going to be done by the computer anyway,
but the intuition can tell you if you've made a mistake somewhere.

The optional programming units were a bit on the easy side for me, but the mathematical proof units were hard.
It's been a long while since I had to $$ \frac{d}{dx} \log(ex) $$.
I liked doing them though.

The customer support is quick to reply, even though I paid absolutely nothing for the course.
Halfway trough the course the interface changed and it erased all my progress.
I wrote customer support and they answered the next day that it was fixed.

Overall, I'm pretty happy with the course.
I'm going to start the next one tomorrow!
