I'm planning on starting Udacity's [Machine Learning Engineer Nanodegree](https://www.udacity.com/course/machine-learning-engineer-nanodegree--nd009).

Before entering the program I'm brushing up my skills by taking the following courses:

* [Intro to Statistics](https://www.udacity.com/course/intro-to-statistics--st101)
* [Linear Algebra Refresher Course](https://www.udacity.com/course/linear-algebra-refresher-course--ud953)

Initially I was actually looking for a course about Data Science. I took the the quiz on [80000hours](https://80000hours.org/)
after listening to [Tim Ferriss's podcast with Will MacAskill](http://fourhourworkweek.com/2015/11/22/will-macaskill/)
and Data Science was a top pick for me.

On first impression the mix of programming skills and more theoretical and scientific skills got me excited.
But after looking into it more deeply I figured that a data scientist was more of a statistician with programming experience,
whereas machine learning is for programmers with some statistical knowledge. So machine learning was a more natural fit for me.

Also, Udacity looks much better than some of the record-once-and-forget MOOCs available online.
Where most other sites have you wait until the semester starts in university if you want any kind of feedback (or grades),
Udacity promises monthly enrollment with code reviews and personal coaching. 

I will keep the blog updated with my progress and impressions of the program.
